Who I am:

Allow me to introduce myself; My name is Jesse Baker. This is a blog dedicated to my journey becomming a developer consisting of how I started, whatI am learning, and what I aim to achieve. I am hoping this document will serveas a future reference to my former timid self, and a reminder that setting time aside for personal growth introduces new and exciting opportunites.

How I started

Like many, I became interested in computers at a young age. Initially I was always outside with friends, but as I grew so did the level of punishment for my acting out. Eventually I was in a perpetual state of 'all work and no play', trapped in a house alone with a computer. We had these things called Macs at school, but they just looked like mini televisions if you ask me. I remember playing with the paint program a few times, especially the turtle stamps; we would paste them all over the place, resizing, copying, coloring. It was mindless entertainment but it taught me a lot about how to manuever with a mouse and a keyboard. I was 5 or 6 then, and I was around 7 or 8 when I started messing with my home computer. This was not a Mac. However, it was an autocad machine. My mom was a architectural student at the time and she was always on this thing when she wasn't physcially in class or at work (usually she was using this computer on the weekends or after 7-8p.m. during the week). For about 3 hours a day after school, since I was not to leave the house, I would sit on the computer. Granted I did not know excatly how things worked, nor did I have anyone to teach me, but I have always had a tendency to learn by exlporing and experimenting. Not to brag, but as a result of all of this time behind a keyboard I became quite proficient with search engines. It taught me how to access music for free ( sorry copyright laws ) and, in a pathetic attempt, to download and play pc games. Had I not crashed my mothers computer 5 or 6 times, I would probably never have learned these valuable lessons: 1.You can find anything on the internet with the right parameters; 2. don't crash your moms school computer becuase her midterms are more important than pirating warcraft.
